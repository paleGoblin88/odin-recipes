Miso Soup

Homemade Miso Soup Recipe

Miso soup is a traditional Japanese soup made from a fermented soybean paste called miso. It is typically made by dissolving miso in a dashi (a Japanese soup stock made from kelp and bonito fish flakes) and adding ingredients such as tofu, green onions, and wakame (a type of seaweed). Miso soup can be served as a side dish with a meal or as a main dish with the addition of protein such as seafood or meat. It is considered a healthy and nutritious food, as it is low in calories and high in vitamins and minerals.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped wakame
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons dashi granules
  • 3 tablespoons miso paste
  • 4 ounces silken tofu, cubed
  • 2 green onions, sliced on the bias

Steps

  1. Place wakame in a fine-mesh sieve and soak in some cold water for 10 minutes.
  2. Combine 4 cups water and dashi granules in a saucepan and bring to a boil over medium heat. Add miso paste and whisk to dissolve. Add wakame and simmer for 3 minutes.
  3. Divide tofu between 4 serving bowls. Ladle miso soup on top and garnish with green onions.
